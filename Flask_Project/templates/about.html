Arch Genome

Project Overview and Objectives

Welcome to Arch Genome! Explore our user-friendly web application designed for the comprehensive analysis of genetic data. Collaborating with a clinician who collected samples in Siberia and integrated data based on chromosome 1 (CHR1) from the 1000 Genomes Project, our app specializes in admixture, clustering, and population differentiation analyses. Users can select specific populations or superpopulations, gaining insights into genetic relationships. The app also facilitates the retrieval of allele and genotype frequencies for SNPs of interest, providing valuable clinical relevance information. With informative plots and analyses, our tool is dedicated to answering key biological questions. Utilizing SQL, our code is available on GitHub as a working prototype, laying the foundation for future development by professionals.

Objective Techniques Description
Admixture Analysis ADMIXTURE Leveraging established algorithms to quantify ancestral components, shedding light on historical gene flow and population mixing dynamics.
Clustering Analysis PCA Employing cutting-edge clustering techniques to discern intricate patterns of relatedness among subgroups within the sampled populations.
Population Differentiation Analysis Weir and Cockerham F-statistics Producing a comprehensive matrix of pairwise population genetic differentiation, accompanied by visually intuitive representations, to discern genetic divergence among selected populations.
SNP Information ANNOVAR, VCFtools Facilitating the retrieval of sample allele and genotype frequencies, coupled with clinical relevance information, for Single Nucleotide Polymorphisms (SNPs) of interest. This exploration extends to genomic regions, genes, and SNPs, allowing users to customize their analyses.

Accessing the Genetic Code

Users can download the genetic data and annotation file from this link. For more detailed information, kindly refer to our comprehensive documentation on GitHub. Our application, version 1.0, integrates genomic data from the hg38refGene dataset (last updated on August 17, 2020, at UCSC) and the hg38clinvar dataset (with data up to December 31, 2022). Additionally, we collaborate with genetic researchers, and our genetic dataset, comprising chr1.vcf.gz and sample_pop.tsv files, was last updated on January 18, 2024. We are committed to providing you with the latest and most comprehensive genomic information in our app.
